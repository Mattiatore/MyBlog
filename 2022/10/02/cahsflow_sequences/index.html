Cashflow Sequences

2022-10-02

If you borrow the amount P (called the principal), which must be repaid after a time T along with simple interest at rate r per time T, then the amount to be repaid at time T is

P+rP=P(1+r)P + rP = P(1+r)P+rP=P(1+r)

From the point of view of a lender instead the present value of a payoff of v to be received at time period k, given a a nominal rate r per period, is

v(1+r)−kv(1+r)^{-k}v(1+r)−k

Suppose that you are to receive payments at the end of each of the next five years. Which of the following three payment sequences is preferable?

One question that arises naturally is that given two cashflow sequences

b1,b2,…,bn and c1,c2,…,cnb_1, b_2, \ldots, b_n \quad \text { and } c_1, c_2, \ldots, c_nb1​,b2​,…,bn​ and c1​,c2​,…,cn​

Under what conditions is the present value of the first sequence at least as large as that of the second for every positive interest rate r ?

Sufficient Conditions
A trivial sufficient condition is

bi≥ci∀i∈[1,n]b_i \ge c_i \hspace{10px} \forall i \in [1,n]bi​≥ci​∀i∈[1,n]

However there exist weaker sufficient conditions. Let

Bi=∑j=1ibj and Ci=∑j=1icj∀i∈[1,n]B_i=\sum_{j=1}^i b_j \quad \text { and } \quad C_i=\sum_{j=1}^i c_j \hspace{10px} \forall i \in [1,n]Bi​=∑j=1i​bj​ and Ci​=∑j=1i​cj​∀i∈[1,n]

Then a sufficient condition is

Bi≥Ci∀i∈[1,n]B_i \ge C_i \hspace{10px} \forall i \in [1,n]Bi​≥Ci​∀i∈[1,n]

Proof Sketch

Bn≥Cn⇒(1+r)−nBn≥(1+r)−nCn⇒(1+r)−n(Bn−1+bn)≥(1+r)−n(Bn−1+bn)B_n \ge C_n \hspace{10px} \Rightarrow \hspace{10px} (1+r)^{-n}B_n \ge (1+r)^{-n}C_n \hspace{10px} \Rightarrow \\[5pt] (1+r)^{-n}(B_{n-1} + b_n) \ge (1+r)^{-n}(B_{n-1} + b_n)Bn​≥Cn​⇒(1+r)−nBn​≥(1+r)−nCn​⇒(1+r)−n(Bn−1​+bn​)≥(1+r)−n(Bn−1​+bn​)

Then we note that

Bn−1≥Cn−1⇒aBn−1≥aCn−1∀a∈R+B_{n-1}\ge C_{n-1} \hspace{10px} \Rightarrow \hspace{10px} aB_{n-1} \ge aC_{n-1} \hspace{10px} \forall a \in R^+Bn−1​≥Cn−1​⇒aBn−1​≥aCn−1​∀a∈R+

Summing the two disequalities

Bn−1(a+(1+r)−n)+(1+r)−nbn≥Cn−1(a+(1+r)−n)+(1+r)−ncnB_{n-1}(a+(1+r)^{-n}) + (1+r)^{-n}b_n \ge C_{n-1}(a+(1+r)^{-n}) + (1+r)^{-n}c_nBn−1​(a+(1+r)−n)+(1+r)−nbn​≥Cn−1​(a+(1+r)−n)+(1+r)−ncn​

For a particular value of

a=(1+r)−n+1−(1+r)−n>0a = (1+r)^{-n+1} - (1+r)^{-n} > 0a=(1+r)−n+1−(1+r)−n>0

We get that the coefficients of the last two indices are the same as in the present value equation, therefore iterating this process would completes the proof.
